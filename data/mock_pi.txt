VERALIX (veralixumab) 150 mg/mL injection, for subcutaneous use
Initial U.S. Approval: 2023

--- INDICATIONS AND USAGE ---
Veralix is a humanized monoclonal antibody indicated for the treatment of
adults with moderate to severe chronic inflammatory condition (CIC) who have
had an inadequate response to conventional therapy. Veralix works by binding
to and neutralizing interleukin-23 (IL-23), reducing the inflammatory cascade
that drives CIC symptoms. Veralix is intended for long-term maintenance
therapy under the supervision of a healthcare provider.

--- DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION ---
The recommended dose of Veralix is 150 mg administered by subcutaneous
injection every 2 weeks. The first dose may be given by a healthcare provider;
subsequent doses may be self-administered after training. Rotate injection
sites (thigh, abdomen, or upper arm). Do not inject into skin that is tender,
bruised, red, or hard. If a dose is missed, administer it as soon as you
remember, then resume the every-2-week schedule. Do not take two doses to make
up for a missed dose. Do not change your dose or stop Veralix without speaking
to your doctor.

--- WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS ---
Serious infections: Veralix may lower the ability to fight infections. Do not
start Veralix during an active infection. Hypersensitivity reactions,
including anaphylaxis, have been reported. Seek emergency care immediately for
signs of a serious allergic reaction such as difficulty breathing, swelling of
the face or throat, severe rash, or chest tightness. Patients should be
up to date on immunizations before starting therapy. Live vaccines should be
avoided during treatment.

--- ADVERSE REACTIONS ---
The most common adverse reactions (incidence >=3%) are injection site
reactions (redness, swelling, itching), headache, upper respiratory tract
infections, fatigue, and nausea. Most injection site reactions are mild to
moderate and resolve within a few days. Contact your healthcare provider if
reactions persist or worsen.

--- STORAGE AND HANDLING ---
Store Veralix refrigerated at 2 C to 8 C (36 F to 46 F) in the original carton
to protect from light. Do not freeze. Do not shake. If needed, Veralix may be
kept at room temperature (up to 25 C / 77 F) for a single period of up to 14
days; discard if not used within that window. Keep out of reach of children.

--- PATIENT COUNSELING INFORMATION ---
Advise patients to read the FDA-approved Medication Guide. Counsel patients on
proper subcutaneous injection technique and safe disposal of needles in a
sharps container. Instruct patients to report signs of infection (fever,
chills, persistent cough) or allergic reaction to their healthcare provider.
Remind patients to maintain their dosing schedule for best results and to
consult their doctor before stopping therapy or receiving any vaccine.
